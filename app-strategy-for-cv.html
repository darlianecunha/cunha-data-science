App Strategy to Strengthen the CV

Portfolio roadmap for building a stronger Sustainability Data Scientist profile through applied maritime AI, emissions analytics, decision support and reproducible data products.

Recommended flagship app: Port Decarbonisation Scenario Planner

This should be the next high-impact portfolio application because it connects the strongest parts of the current profile: maritime decarbonisation, CO₂ modelling, SDG analytics, port operations, data engineering and applied machine learning.

The app would transform port call data into practical decisions by estimating baseline emissions, forecasting future emissions, comparing OPS and fuel scenarios, calculating avoided CO₂, and ranking investment priorities by environmental impact and feasibility.

Why this improves the CV

1. It moves the portfolio beyond dashboards

The current portfolio already shows strong dashboards, calculators, text mining and computer vision. A scenario planner would show product thinking, forecasting, optimisation and policy-relevant decision support.

2. It demonstrates applied ML with domain value

The app can include predictive models for emissions by cargo type, vessel profile, berth time and operational conditions, making the data science component clearer to recruiters.

3. It creates a strong interview story

The project can be explained as an end-to-end data product: raw port data, modelling assumptions, validation, interactive scenarios, user outputs and reproducible GitHub documentation.

Suggested app scope
Module What it does CV signal
Baseline emissions Calculates annual, monthly and per-call CO₂ emissions from auxiliary engines at berth. Carbon accounting, port emissions, IMO-based methodology.
Forecasting model Predicts future emissions under cargo growth, berth-time variation and vessel profile changes. Machine learning, regression, model evaluation, feature engineering.
OPS scenario Compares emissions with and without onshore power supply using electricity emission factors and adoption rates. Climate analytics, energy transition, scenario modelling.
Investment prioritisation Ranks berths or cargo groups by avoided emissions, operational feasibility and implementation priority. Decision intelligence, analytics for managers, applied optimisation.
Executive report Generates a downloadable summary with methods, charts, assumptions and recommendations. Communication, stakeholder reporting, reproducible outputs.

Portfolio card ready to use

Port Decarbonisation Scenario Planner

Purpose: A decision-support application that estimates baseline at-berth CO₂ emissions, forecasts future emissions and compares decarbonisation scenarios such as OPS adoption, cleaner fuels and berth-priority strategies.

How to use: Upload or select port call data, define operational assumptions and compare scenarios through emissions, avoided CO₂, carbon-cost savings and implementation priority.

Who it is for: Port authorities, terminal operators, sustainability teams, researchers and climate-policy analysts working on maritime decarbonisation.

Efficiency gain: Converts technical emissions data into investment-ready scenarios and clear management recommendations, reducing manual spreadsheet modelling.

Suggested TRL: TRL 6 after a working prototype with real data and documented validation.

CV bullet ready to use
